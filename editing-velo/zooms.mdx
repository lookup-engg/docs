### **1. Adding Automatic Zooms to a Velo Video**

Velo does not automatically generate new zoom points once the video has
been synthesised. However, you can control the behavior of existing
zooms using **Zoom mode**. In\
**Auto** mode, the camera will automatically move to maintain visibility
of your mouse cursor as it navigates the page.

### **2. Zoom Level Adjustment**

Within the Zoom editor, use the **Zoom Level** slider to adjust how
closely the camera focuses on your screen.

- You can set a specific zoom percentage (e.g., **50%**) to ensure clear
  visibility.

- If you want to return to the standard view, simply click the **Reset**
  button next to the slider.

### **3. Manual Zoom Functionality on Velo**

By switching the **Zoom mode** to **Manual**, you gain the ability to
lock the camera onto a specific area of your video.

- This allows you to highlight a specific UI element or section of the
  page, even if the cursor moves elsewhere.

- You can select the exact portion of the frame you want to zoom into
  directly on the preview window.

### **4. Adjusting Zoom Effects in Velo**

You have several options to customize how transitions appear:

- **Instant Animation:** Toggle this switch to make the camera jump
  immediately to the zoomed-in view rather than using a smooth
  transition.

- **Disable Zoom:** If you want to keep your zoom settings but prevent
  them from appearing in the final video, enable the **Disable zoom**
  toggle. The zoom layer will remain saved, but the camera will stay at
  a standard distance.
