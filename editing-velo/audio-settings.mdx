### **1. Adding Background Music to a Velo**

You can add background music by enabling the **Background Music** toggle
at the top of the menu. Velo provides a library of tracks, which you can
preview and select to set the right mood for your walkthrough.

### **2. Background Music Upload Functionality**

The current version of the Velo editor does not support custom music
uploads. You can choose from the pre-selected library available in the
**Audio** tab.

![Description](/images/editing-velo/audio-settings/1.png)

### **3. Adjusting Background Music Volume**

Once background music is enabled, use the **Background Music Volume**
slider to find the perfect balance. This allows the music to play softly
in the background without overpowering your voice.

![Description](/images/editing-velo/audio-settings/2.png)

### **4. Adjusting the Master Volume on Velo**

The **Master Volume** slider allows you to increase or decrease the
overall sound level of your entire project. This affects everything from
your voice clone to the background music simultaneously.

![Description](/images/editing-velo/audio-settings/3.png)

### **5. Adjusting Narration Volume After Velo Creation**

You can adjust your voice clone\'s loudness specifically using the
**Narration Volume** slider. This is helpful if you want your voice to
be more prominent over the system audio or music.

![Description](/images/editing-velo/audio-settings/4.png)

### **6. System Audio Management Post-Velo Creation**

Velo allows for the management of system audio settings even after a
Velo is generated.

If your screen recording captured unwanted sounds from your computer,
you can enable the **Mute System Audio** toggle. Alternatively, you can
use the **System Audio** slider to lower the volume of those specific
sounds without muting them completely.

![Description](/images/editing-velo/audio-settings/5.png)
