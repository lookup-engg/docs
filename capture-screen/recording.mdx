Once you click **Share** in the Chrome screen picker, Velo starts recording immediately. The modal updates to show the recording-in-progress state: a red **● REC** indicator, a **Recording in progress** message, and a **Stop Recording** button.

{/* Screenshot: Recording in progress modal */}

## While you're recording

There's no script to follow — just navigate through your product as you normally would. Velo captures every click, scroll, and screen change.

A few things to keep in mind:

**Go at a natural pace.** Don't rush through screens. Velo uses the timing of your recording to place zooms and transitions, so pausing briefly on important UI elements helps the final demo feel intentional.

**Show the key moments.** Think about what your viewer needs to see. Focus on the parts of your product that are most compelling — the "aha" moments.

**Don't worry about mistakes.** You can trim, cut, and crop in the editor after generation. If you navigate to the wrong place, just back up and carry on — it can be cleaned up later.

**Avoid sensitive information.** If your product contains private data, navigate away from it before recording or use a demo account.

## Stopping the recording

When you've captured everything you need, click **Stop Recording** in the modal.

Velo will immediately begin saving your recording. The modal updates to show a spinner and the message **Saving recording…** — this usually takes just a few seconds depending on the length of your recording.

Once saving is complete, you'll be taken to the [Select Voice](/capture-screen/select-voice) page to choose your VeloTwin and generate your demo.
