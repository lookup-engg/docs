---
title: "Generating a Doc"
description: "Automatically transform your video content into written documentation."
---

Velo allows you to automatically transform video content into written documentation, providing a readable companion for your recordings that captures every UI action and insight.

## Access the Docs interface

Navigate to the top-right corner of the Velo interface, next to the **Video** preview, and select the **Docs** tab. If a document has not been created for the current project yet, the screen will display a "No doc yet" status.

<Frame>
  ![Docs tab selection](/images/docs/generating-a-doc/scene-2.png)
</Frame>

The Docs tab is located at the top right of the preview panel.

## Generate the document

Click the **Generate Doc** button. The system will process the video content, a task that typically takes a few seconds. During this time, you will see a "Generating your doc..." status message.

<Frame>
  ![Generate Doc button](/images/docs/generating-a-doc/scene-3.png)
</Frame>

Initiate document creation using the Generate Doc button.

## Review and edit content

Once the document appears, you can perform manual edits to refine the text. Both the title and the body of the doc are fully editable to ensure the final output meets your specific needs. The generated document includes detailed annotations and captions that correspond to specific actions within the video.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot 2026 05 26 At 1 38 46 PM](/images/Screenshot-2026-05-26-at-1.38.46-PM.png)
</Frame>

Review and edit the generated headings, text, and screenshots.

## Share and export

After verifying the content and making any necessary formatting changes, click **Save** to preserve your edits. To distribute the document:

1. Click the **Share** button in the top-right corner.
2. Select your preferred export format from the **Share Velo** modal.
3. Choose **PDF** for a fixed-layout document or **Markdown** for use in other documentation tools.

<Frame>
  ![Share and export options](/images/docs/generating-a-doc/scene-14.png)
</Frame>

Export the final document as a PDF or Markdown file.

The resulting document provides an easy-to-read, detailed walkthrough that serves as a permanent reference for the video content.
